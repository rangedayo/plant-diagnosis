issue_type: Sudden Leaf Drop

symptoms:
* Sudden dropping of leaves
* Losing a few leaves in the spring
* Losing many leaves in the fall

cause:
* Abrupt changes in growing environment
* Seasonal changes in light levels
* Under-fertilizing or under-watering
* Spider mite infestation

solution:
* Allow plants to adapt slowly to new conditions
* Maintain proper watering and fertilization schedules
* Inspect for and control spider mites


issue_type: Slow Loss of Lower Leaves

symptoms:
* Gradual dropping of the lowest leaves
* Loss of oldest leaves at the base of the plant

cause:
* Low level of soil fertility
* Nutrient translocation from old leaves to new growth points

solution:
* Repot the plant if necessary
* Start a regular fertilizer program


issue_type: Marginal or Tip Leaf Burn

symptoms:
* Burning of leaf margins or tips
* Production of smaller leaves
* Discolored or damaged leaf edges

cause:
* Over-fertilization
* Under-watering
* High fluoride levels in water or potting soil
* Salt buildup in soil from poor quality water

solution:
* Repot the plant with fresh potting soil
* Use scissors to reshape and trim burned tips
* Improve water quality if using high-salt or hard water
* Adjust watering and fertilization frequency


issue_type: Leaves Small and Off-Color

symptoms:
* New leaves appearing smaller than normal
* Leaves showing pale or unusual coloration
* Visible signs of poor plant vigor

cause:
* Lack of essential nutrients (poor nutrition)
* Root death caused by over-watering
* Inconsistent feeding schedule

solution:
* Begin a regular monthly liquid fertilizer program
* Reduce fertilization frequency during low-light winter days
* Adjust watering habits to prevent root damage


issue_type: Plants Don’t Grow

symptoms:
* Lack of new growth
* Plant remaining stagnant in the same state
* Failure to begin growth after relocation

cause:
* Insufficient light levels
* Need for adjustment period to a new environment
* Light levels lower than the minimum required for photosynthesis

solution:
* Move the plant to a brighter location
* Place the plant closer to a window
* Ensure light is bright enough to read a newspaper
* Allow sufficient time for the plant to adapt to its environment


issue_type: Plants Don’t Flower

symptoms:
* Absence of blooms or flowering
* Failure to produce flowers during the expected season
* Normal plant functions maintained without flower production

cause:
* Insufficient light accumulation to store energy (photo-accumulators)
* Lack of specific environmental signals or seasonal triggers
* Inadequate day length or light duration
* Failure to meet specific plant-specific blooming conditions

solution:
* Move plants to brighter conditions to increase energy storage
* Provide sufficient time for photo-accumulators to build energy
* Research and provide specific environmental triggers for seasonal bloomers
* Ensure the plant's unique blooming requirements are met based on its species


issue_type: Tips of Fig Tree Dying

symptoms:
* Death of youngest twigs
* Visible blight at the tips of branches

cause:
* Infection by Phomes tip blight pathogen
* High humidity conditions

solution:
* Prune out affected branches several inches below the infection zone
* Disinfect pruning shears in a 10% bleach solution between cuts


issue_type: Leaves Mottled on the Upper Surface

symptoms:
* Yellow/green-mottled appearance on leaf surfaces
* Tiny dust-sized insects crawling on leaves
* Visible webbing in severe cases

cause:
* Infestation by two-spotted spider mites
* Mites scraping mouthparts across the leaf surface
* Warm and dry environmental conditions
* Use of ineffective insecticides that worsen populations

solution:
* Spray with insecticidal soap twice a week for two to three weeks
* Use ultrafine horticultural oil
* Mist the plant with warm, soapy water
* Relocate to cool, moist, or high-humidity areas to hamper mites


issue_type: Leaves Distorted With the Edges Curled Down 

symptoms:
* New leaves curled at the edges and twisted
* Extremely distorted growing terminals
* Visible deformity in the plant's growing point

cause:
* Infestation by Cyclamen mites or broad mites
* Mites feeding directly in the growing point of the plant

solution:
* Destroy infested plants to prevent further spread
* Isolate susceptible species like African violets and English ivy


issue_type: Plants Have White Globs on the Stems

symptoms:
* White, meal-like globs at the juncture of petiole and stem
* Small white insects about the size of a match head
* Immature insects crawling slowly on the plant
* Presence of immobile white insects at maturity

cause:
* Infestation by mealybugs
* Insects sucking juices from the plant stems

solution:
* Physically kill insects using an alcohol-soaked cotton swab
* Apply a fine grade of horticultural oil with thorough coverage
* Use systemic insecticides like Orthene or Bayer Advanced during active growth
* Avoid use on ferns as they are not susceptible to these pests


issue_type: Floor Is Sticky

symptoms:
* Sticky goo (honeydew) on the floor, table, or leaves
* Presence of brown, turtle-shaped shells about 1/16 inch long
* Small bumps blending in with woody stems

cause:
* Infestation by brown hemispherical scale insects
* Secretion of "honeydew" (insect feces) while feeding
* Insects piercing and sucking juices from the plant

solution:
* Apply lightweight horticultural oil
* Use systemic insecticides such as Orthene or Bayer Advanced
* Inspect woody stems closely for hidden insects


issue_type: Small Gnats Buzzing Around Plant

symptoms:
* Small, black, gnat-like insects buzzing around the soil
* Presence of adult fungus gnats or shore flies
* Larvae found in the potting soil

cause:
* Larvae feeding on organic debris and decaying matter in soil
* Moist potting soil providing a habitat for fungus gnats
* Natural life cycle of the adult gnat form

solution:
* Drench the soil with an insecticide such as malathion if they become a nuisance
* Scout and quarantine new plants before bringing them home
* Read and follow insecticide label recommendations carefully
* Spray plants outdoors and allow foliage to dry completely before bringing them back inside
